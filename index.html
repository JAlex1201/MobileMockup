Meet Maya

28, ex-Target marketing, now building her own agency.

Maya Okafor is a 28-year-old Twin Cities entrepreneur. She started out as a marketing specialist at Target, and she's now spinning up her own agency, Aurora Studio. She's at Startup Week to network, see what other founders are building, and sit in on the AI and FinTech sessions, and most of all to connect with local VCs about what it really takes to be a successful founder.

🤝 Networking 🤖 AI sessions 💳 FinTech sessions 📈 Meeting local VCs
Feature · Session Catalogue

Tuesday, 6:00 AM. Coffee's on, and Maya opens the session catalogue.

First full day of sessions, and she's up early: dog out, coffee made, phone in hand. She filled most of her week last week, but a few open slots are still waiting. Keynotes and morning networking are already on her Agenda; everything else is hers to pick.

A catalogue, not a timetable

Sessions shows every available session as a browseable list, filtered by day and tagged by topic track. Keynotes and morning networking are pre-loaded on every attendee's Agenda. Everything else is opt-in: Maya taps in, reads the description and speakers, and fills her last few open slots if seats remain.

  • Keynotes and morning networking auto-added to every Agenda, no action needed
  • Day tabs (Sept 14–18) switch the view instantly; sessions show in time order
  • Topic track tags (AI & Tech, FinTech, Demo Lab, Capital…) for fast scanning
  • Seat count on every session; sign-up closes when the room fills

Connect · Opt-in Directory

Community keeps sponsors visible. Connect keeps founders findable.

The Community tab gives every sponsor a full profile page: browse by tier or track, tap in, see who they are and how to reach them. The Connect directory is entirely opt-in: only attendees who choose to be discoverable appear, and they share only the contact methods they want others to use.

  • Community tab: dedicated profiles for every sponsor, filterable by tier and track
  • Sponsors self-manage their profile, with no BETA staff writing copy for 30 companies
  • Connect directory is 100% opt-in, and nobody appears who hasn't published their profile
  • Each profile shows only the contact methods the attendee chose to share

Badge · Eventbrite Sync

One badge. Five days. Three campuses.

Attendees check in with the QR code Eventbrite already gives them. The app pulls that ticket via Eventbrite's API and saves the details straight to the attendee's profile, so if Maya needs to step out, she pulls up the same QR to be scanned, whether she opens Eventbrite or TCSW.

  • Eventbrite owns purchase, refunds, and ticket data, no parallel store to maintain
  • App syncs ticket state via Eventbrite API: paid, checked-in, transferred, refunded
  • No Apple Wallet or Google Wallet passes (one less surface to support and approve)
  • Presenting sponsor visibility on the personal badge surface (one slot, capped)

What's new for BETA Owned

The pieces BETA owns and reuses year over year:

  • One TCSW web app codebase, deployed to Vercel (or similar)
  • Supabase project: sessions, speakers, sponsors, RSVPs, profiles
  • TCSW design system: navy + gold, diagonal stripes, MN-arrow brand
  • Sponsor dashboard with page-view counts (Judd's flag-on-load idea)

What we don't have to build Trusted

The pieces we let trusted services handle, so we don't reinvent them:

  • Eventbrite: ticketing, refunds, on-site QR check-in
  • Capacitor: iOS & Android packaging, push notifications, store submission
  • Mapbox: street data, search, walking directions
  • Supabase Auth: sign-in via email or Eventbrite SSO
